The old bookstore on Elm Street had been there for as long as anyone could remember, its weathered sign creaking gently in the wind. Inside, the smell of aged paper and leather bindings filled the air, while dust motes danced in the slivers of sunlight filtering through the tall windows. Shelves stretched from floor to ceiling, crammed with volumes of every shape and size—some with gilded lettering, others worn and faded by countless readers over the decades. In the back corner, near the fireplace, an orange tabby cat slept peacefully on a stack of poetry books, occasionally twitching its whiskers as if dreaming of literary adventures. The elderly owner, Mr. Henderson, could often be found behind the counter, peering through his spectacles at some rare first edition, always ready to recommend the perfect book to anyone who walked through his door.
